Beach #2: Malapascua Island

Many people know that people come to Cebu for its amazing history, churches, and parks, but few people know that people also travel to Cebu for its white beaches and gorgeous nature. Malapascua is located 6.8 kilometers off the northern coast of Cebu Island. Malapascua Island has drawn both local and foreign visitors, particularly divers, due to its abundant marine sanctuary. It is most recognized for its world-famous diving locations, particularly the Monad Shoal, where thresher sharks can be spotted and watched before daybreak on a regular basis.

See the Lighthouse beach

Situated fairly close to the Lighthouse Beach is Guimbitayan Beach. Perfect for relaxation, this bach is hidden behind many fishermen houses along the way.

If you want the entire beach for yourself, Langub Beach is the place you should visit for it is a very quiet beach in the Northen part of Malapascua.
